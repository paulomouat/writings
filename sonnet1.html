Sonnet I
© 1998 Paulo Mouat
Morning: So bright and warm it is today
But brighter and warmer shines my love still;
Upon the dewy mantle she does lay,
Heavenly as a dream but indeed real.
Among the blossoms of this meadow, green
Of leaves and petals and a streaming brook
She's the gentlest, for whose fragrance and sheen
My heart has bloomed and I was swiftly took.
Treading barefoot upon this hazy knoll,
Lightly and merrily singing a song,
My darling flower, she looks like a doll,
A beauty in white, and for her I long.
My love is a rose, lovely and slender,
My heart is her garden, wide and tender.
